# Abstract

Soft skin-interfacing electronics require ion-conductive materials that remain mechanically compliant during repeated deformation [1].
	柔性皮肤界面电子器件需要在反复变形过程中仍保持力学顺应性的离子导电材料 [1]。
However, many gels lose interfacial stability when exposed to sweat or prolonged strain [2,3].
	然而，许多凝胶在暴露于汗液或长期应变时会失去界面稳定性 [2,3]。
Here, we describe a model elastomeric conductor that combines reversible bonding with a hydrophobic matrix.
	在此，我们描述一种模型弹性导体，其将可逆键合与疏水基体相结合。


The material maintains conductivity after cyclic stretching and supports stable electrophysiological recording.
	该材料在循环拉伸后仍能保持导电性，并支持稳定的电生理记录。
These results suggest that molecular-level energy dissipation can improve the durability of soft bioelectronic interfaces [4].
	这些结果表明，分子尺度的能量耗散能够提升软生物电子界面的耐久性 [4]。


# Introduction

Hydrogels have been widely used as soft ionic interfaces because their water-rich networks resemble biological tissue [5].
	水凝胶因其富水网络类似生物组织，已被广泛用作柔性离子界面 [5]。
Their practical use is nevertheless limited by dehydration in air and swelling in aqueous environments.
	然而，其实际应用仍受到空气中脱水和水环境中溶胀的限制。
